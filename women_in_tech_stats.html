Employers are doing a lot to try and recruit more women but the numbers aren't there. Why?

Employers can only interview and recruit what is in front of them. Tech employers advertise the majority of their roles. Look at any tech job that's advertised and you will see the majority of companies have a diversity statement stating clearly they don't discriminate on any grounds, including gender and they actively encourage applications from women.

The problem lies with not enough women going into stem/tech at a young age

Source: PwC

To dissect the problem, we need to look at early development. In high school, girls achieve better grades than boys. Yet for girls who do pursue computer science at university level, they find themselves being outnumbered by males “52% vs 30% “ one of the highest gender disparities in course subjects. And this imbalance isn't helped by the falling trend of females taking up science, maths and computing courses.

The field of computing actually used to be dominated by women.

Source: BBC

But there are no women in tech to look up to right?........Wrong!

There is a current narrative that there are no women in tech to look up to, and a misconception that women don't succeed in tech. This is not true and if anything, this narrative is damaging and hurting women before they even get started. Below is a list of women who have not only succeeded in tech, but have reached the absolute pinnacle and are either currently, or have recently headed up billion-dollar multinational organisations.

  1. Sheryl Sandberb- COO of Facebook
  2. Ginny Rmetty - CEO of IBM
  3. Ruth Porat - CFO Google
  4. Julie Sweet - CEO of Accenture
  5. Susan Diane Wojcicki – CEO of YouTube
  6. Reshma Saujani – Founder of Girls Who Code

  8. Marissa Mayer - Formerly president & CEO of Yahoo
  9. Meg Whitman - Previous president & CEO of Hewlett Packard

What are the rewards of a career in tech?

Gemma Church from women in tech wrote recently “Tech is probably the only field that allows you to work on anything from anywhere”

Standard benefits that you can expect with most tech companies are:

  1. Work from home and hybrid working environments
  2. Flexible hours, not glued to a desk from 9am until 5pm
  3. The majority of tech companies offer maternity & paternity leave on full pay
  4. Private medical health insurance
